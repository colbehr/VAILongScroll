The Bottom Line: Older folks don’t want to feel like a burden.

Meet Rose!

Rose recently had a knee replacement, so comfortably getting around in her life is a worry for her right now. Her family doesn’t live close by so wants to remain independent so as not to worry her family.

Hobbies
  • Swimming
  • Painting
  • Walks with Friends
Goals
  • Keep independence for as long as she can
  • Wants to be able to accomplish most chores by herself for as long as possible
Worries
  • Worried about having to start using public transportation since she will not be able to drive soon
  • Coming to terms with being mentally slower, and having her body slow down
  • Has trouble understanding and getting through the healthcare system to get resources she needs

Rose’s User Journey

Rose gets herself ready to go to the doctors but has trouble finding her glasses.

Rose can’t find her glasses, but decides to start driving anyway.

She gets in a fender bender on her way to the doctors.

Rose misses her doctor’s appointment and gets her license taken away.

Possible ideas for improvement

Rideshare, or network geared towards providing transportation

Family alert system, to alert family that they are driving

Improved communication network/safer travel options

REFLECTION

What could we have spent more time developing?

With hindsight, we can see where we could develop our product further and what parts need improvement.
What do we think went well?

We conducted really productive user testing and got some great feedback.

The hardware design and accompanying renders helped bring our idea to life.

Looking back, what could have been done better?

Organizing our prototype frames, assets and labeling from the beginning.

Utilizing our persona’s and story more to show why changes were made.

What would you work on next if you had more time?
  • We heard lots of feedback about onboarding experiences in our user testing, and having more time would have let us explore that more.
  • We wanted to create a working prototype of our remote, however it ended up being too ambitious.
  • Inputting text and information was something we wanted to explore more.
  • Final small design changes (consistent alignments, fonts, and colors).
